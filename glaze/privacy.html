Privacy by architecture, not by promise

Your work never leaves this device.

Glaze is a static web app. There is no Glaze account, no Glaze server that receives your files, and no database. Everything you open, paint, edit and export is processed locally in your browser and discarded when you close the tab.

What we collect

Nothing. We genuinely have no way to collect anything, because there is no backend to send it to.

  • No accounts, sign-ups, emails or profiles.
  • No analytics, telemetry, fingerprinting or tracking pixels.
  • No advertising and no third-party trackers.
  • Your images and videos are read directly from your device into the browser's memory and never transmitted anywhere.

Cookies & storage

Glaze uses no cookies and no local storage of any kind. We don't even remember your light/dark preference between visits — the appearance follows your system setting, and a manual toggle lasts only until you reload.

  • No cookies → no cookie-consent banner is legally required.
  • No localStorage, sessionStorage or IndexedDB writing your data.
  • Your files exist only in this tab's memory and are gone the moment you close or refresh it. Export anything you want to keep.

Where your files go

When you export, the finished file is generated in your browser and handed to your device's normal download mechanism. It is saved wherever your browser saves downloads — on your device, under your control. Glaze never receives a copy.

Optional engines loaded from public CDNs

Two advanced features download code (not your media) from public content-delivery networks the first time you use them:

  • Voxel 3D mode loads the Three.js 3D engine from jsDelivr.
  • Video "Power export" (MP4 / GIF / MOV) loads ffmpeg.wasm from unpkg (~25 MB, once).

Like loading any web page, requesting a file from a CDN reveals your IP address and basic request headers to that CDN — that's how the internet works. It sends none of your media and no personal data. If you never open Voxel mode or Power export, no such request is ever made, and the rest of Glaze works fully offline once the page has loaded.

Verify it yourself

You don't have to take our word for it. Open your browser's developer tools, go to the Network tab, and use the Paint or Image editor: after the page loads you'll see no further network activity, even while editing and exporting. CDN requests appear only if you open Voxel 3D or Video Power export.

Legal

Data-protection laws (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, etc.): these govern how organisations process personal data. Glaze processes no personal data and stores nothing, so there is nothing for us to retain, share, sell or delete on request.

Your responsibility: please only edit media you have the right to use. You are responsible for how you use the files you create.
